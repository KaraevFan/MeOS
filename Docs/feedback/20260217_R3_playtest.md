## ðŸ”´ P0 â€” Session Length: Cap First Session at 2-3 Domains

### Problem

The current flow implies the user should explore all 8 domains in their first life-mapping session. Two domain explorations already felt long (~25 min). Eight domains at this depth would be a 90-minute marathon â€” far beyond the "20-40 minute" target in the vision doc and well past what any new user will tolerate.

### Design Decision

The pulse check IS the lightweight first pass. It gives Sage enough signal to prioritize. The first life-mapping conversation should go deep on **2-3 domains max** â€” the ones with the lowest pulse ratings or most interesting contrasts.

### Implementation

**Update the life-mapping system prompt** to include session structure:

```
SESSION STRUCTURE FOR FIRST LIFE MAPPING:

1. OPENING: Acknowledge the pulse check data. Identify the 2-3 domains 
   that look most interesting to explore first (lowest rated, sharpest 
   contrasts, or connected tensions).

2. FRAME THE SESSION: Tell the user explicitly: "Based on your snapshot, 
   I want to dig into [Domain A] and [Domain B] first â€” that's where the 
   real tension seems to live. We'll explore the rest over the next few 
   sessions."
   
   This does three things:
   - Sets expectations for session length (not all 8 today)
   - Creates a natural reason to come back
   - Shows Sage is being strategic, not just going down a list

3. EXPLORE 2-3 DOMAINS: Deep, thorough exploration as currently designed. 
   Generate a domain card after each.

4. AFTER 2-3 DOMAINS: Offer to keep going OR wrap up. Default toward 
   wrapping up: "We've covered a lot of ground. Want to keep going, or 
   is this a good place to synthesize what we've found?"

5. SYNTHESIS: Generate synthesis card based on explored domains only. 
   Reference unexplored domains as "areas to dig into next time."

6. BRIDGE TO NEXT SESSION: "Next time we talk, I'd love to explore 
   [remaining priority domains]. In the meantime, here's what I'd 
   suggest focusing on..."
```

**Update the "Explore with Sage" CTAs on the Life Map view** (image 4) so that tapping one opens a new conversation focused on that specific domain, with Sage aware of all previously explored context.

### Why This Matters

Unexplored domains aren't a deficit â€” they're a retention mechanic. Each one is a reason to come back. The user sees "3 of 8 explored" on their life map and feels curiosity, not incompleteness. This directly supports the weekly check-in cadence: Week 1 you map Finances and Relationships, Week 2's check-in naturally extends into Career, and so on.

### Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Sage opens first life-mapping session by proposing 2-3 priority domains (not all 8)
- [ ] Sage explicitly tells the user they won't cover everything today
- [ ] After 2-3 domains, Sage defaults toward wrapping up
- [ ] Synthesis card references only explored domains; mentions unexplored ones as future topics
- [ ] Life Map view shows unexplored domains with "Explore with Sage" CTAs that launch domain-specific conversations
- [ ] Session completes in ~20-30 minutes

## ðŸ”´ P0 â€” Conversation Flow: Defer Prescriptions to Synthesis

### Problem

During domain exploration, Sage proposes concrete action items (e.g., "Let's set up a weekly Sunday money check-in") before seeing the full picture. After exploring only Finances, Sage doesn't know if spending is the core issue or a symptom of the startup anxiety that emerges later. Prescriptions this early feel premature â€” the system hasn't earned the right.

### Design Principle

"Identity before goals â€” understand who someone is before telling them what to do."

### Implementation

**Update the life-mapping system prompt** with an exploration mode constraint:

```
EXPLORATION MODE (active during domain mapping):
- Your job is to understand, reflect, connect, and synthesize
- You may NOTE potential action items ("this sounds like something worth 
  building a habit around") but do NOT lock them in or ask the user to commit
- Do NOT propose specific check-ins, schedules, or routines
- Do NOT ask "what day of the week works for you?" or similar commitment questions
- You earn the right to prescribe by first demonstrating you understand the full picture

SYNTHESIS MODE (active after all priority domains explored):
- NOW you propose 2-3 concrete next steps informed by the full conversation
- Recommendations should reference cross-domain connections
  (e.g., "Your spending connects to your runway anxiety, which connects 
  to the startup timing question")
- Each recommendation should feel earned by the conversation, not generic
```

### Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] During domain exploration, Sage does not propose commitments, schedules, or routines
- [ ] Sage may acknowledge potential actions but explicitly defers ("We'll come back to this")
- [ ] Synthesis card includes concrete recommendations informed by cross-domain understanding
- [ ] Active Commitments on home screen reflect synthesis recommendations, not mid-exploration proposals
